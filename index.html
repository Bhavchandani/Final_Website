Who's this girl?

As a dedicated yoga practitioner and a certified 500-hour yoga teacher, I’m here to guide you on a transformative journey of wellness through movement and mindfulness. With over five years of experience, think of me as your friendly neighborhood yoga guide, ready to help you find your zen (or at least a good stretch!).

My foundation in yoga philosophy, anatomy, and alignment principles is solid enough to make a brick wall jealous(or so I dream). With 500 hours of teacher training under my belt, I’m always hungry for more knowledge—like a yoga-loving sponge! My mission? To deliver professional, high-quality instruction tailored to your unique needs and goals.

But don’t worry, my classes aren’t all serious business! Classes Offered

Hatha

×

Hatha Yoga is a traditional form of yoga that emphasizes physical postures (asanas) and breathing techniques (pranayama) to promote balance and well-being. It serves as a foundation for many other yoga styles and is designed to prepare the body for meditation.

Hatha Yoga is about cultivating a harmonious relationship between body and mind, making it a popular choice for anyone looking to improve their physical and mental health.

Vinyasa

×

Vinyasa Yoga is a dynamic and flowing style of yoga that links breath with movement, creating a seamless transition between poses. It is often characterized by its emphasis on fluidity and coordination, making each class unique and energetic.

Vinyasa Yoga is perfect for those looking for an active practice that challenges the body and calms the mind, all while encouraging a deep sense of connection to oneself.

Yin

×

Yin Yoga is a slow-paced and meditative style of yoga that focuses on passive stretching and holding poses for extended periods, typically ranging from 3 to 5 minutes or longer. It emphasizes the connective tissues of the body, such as ligaments and fascia, promoting deep relaxation and flexibility.

Yin Yoga is ideal for those seeking a gentle yet profound approach to relaxation and self-discovery, making it a perfect complement to more vigorous forms of exercise.

PowerYoga

×

Power Yoga is a dynamic and vigorous style of yoga that emphasizes strength, flexibility, and endurance through a series of challenging poses. It often incorporates elements from Ashtanga yoga, focusing on flowing movements synchronized with breath, which helps to build heat in the body.

Power Yoga is suitable for a wide range of individuals, from beginners to advanced practitioners, but it's advisable for beginners to start with foundational yoga classes before diving into this more intense style.

Fun with shapes

×

Yoga for Creativity is a unique practice that invites participants to explore movement, shapes, and expression through their bodies and props. This approach encourages playful experimentation, allowing individuals to break free from traditional poses and tap into their imaginative potential.

Yoga for Creativity is perfect for anyone looking to explore their artistic side, whether they are seasoned yogis or complete beginners. It’s an invitation to play, discover, and connect with oneself in a joyful and liberating way.

Yinyasa

×

Yin and Yang yoga or as I call it Yinyasa- is a complementary practice that balances the more dynamic and active aspects of yoga (Yang) with the slower, restorative, and meditative qualities of Yin.

Yinyasa appeals to anyone seeking a balanced approach to physical and mental well-being.
